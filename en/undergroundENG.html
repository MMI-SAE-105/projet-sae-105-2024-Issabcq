Underground : a song illustrating the unkown future of robotics

By BACQ ISSA published the 01/08/25 at 08:00pm

Swordfishtrombones album cover” de Tom Waits

Tom Waits' song Underground, from his album Swordfishtrombones (1983), stands out for its strange, experimental sound universe, reflecting themes linked to the future of robotics and artificial intelligence (AI). Its raw style, with metallic percussion and industrial sounds, made it an apt choice for the soundtrack to the animated film Robots (2005).

Robot movie poster

Underground evokes a subterranean world where mechanical entities evolve in the shadows. This imagery corresponds to the world of robotics, where autonomous machines work in the background to build a technological future. The percussive elements and irregular rhythm of the song recall the movements of gears and automated devices. This mechanical atmosphere suggests a future dominated by machines, where AI could redefine the boundaries between human and machine.

Robot movie scene

The choice of Underground for the soundtrack of Robots is not insignificant. The film depicts a world where robots are the norm, populating a universe powered by sophisticated mechanisms. It explores themes linked to the sentience of machines and their ability to sense and interact with their environment. Tom Waits' music, with its raw, unconventional feel, captures this essence and harmonizes with the film's aesthetic.

Rodney Copperbottom & Fender Peenwheeler, main characters

The avant-garde character of Underground, with its dark tones, reflects a vision of robotics where the unknown comes to the fore. The song evokes conscious machines that, like the characters in the film Robots, question their place in a future where AIs are becoming increasingly autonomous. This thematic link between the music and the film's plot allows us to explore the evolution of the concept of sentience in robotics, a topical issue in debates on AI.
